About Me

Hi, I'm Nick Quinlan. I live in Seattle, WA and am Chief Operating Officer at Major League Hacking. Previously, I was a SendGrid Developer Evangelist.

I've been using (or more like been glued to) computers since the age of three, just ask my parents. At age five, I may have had one of the most well planned and marketed lemonade stands a five year old has ever had.

Since then, I've been using my love of technology, business, and lemonade to lead communities, educational initiatives, and technical organizations.

Work & Volunteering

I'm passionate about the work I do, here's I'm up to and where I've been.

  • Work

  • Major League Hacking, Chief Operating Officer

    2014 - Present

    Major League Hacking (MLH) is a Certified B-Corp with a mission to empower hackers. Together with an amazing team and community of over 750,000 hackers, we create life-changing experiences that introduce students to technology and help them throughout their career. In my nearly ten years at the company, I've grown to be Chief Operating Officer, managing the delivery of our core product lines, the team, finances, and the operations of the business.

  • SendGrid, Developer Evangelist

    2013 - 2014

    As a Developer Evangelist at SendGrid, on one of the best regarded developer relations teams in the industry, I promoted SendGrid's developer focused product by building our community, writing technical documentation, developing sample applications, and helping to maintain client libraries. Inside the company I helped to bring product feedback to the relevant teams, focused product development, assisted in supporting our users and helped our enterprise sales team sell the product to customers.

  • Ethoseo, Web Programmer

    2011 - 2013

    At Ethoseo I worked as a web programmer, developing technical solutions to marketing problems, helping SMBs and agencies create and implement successful inbound marketing strategy.

  • Volunteering

  • Washington Business Week, Company Advisor & Incoming Chair

    2022 - Present

    Washington Business Week (WBW) is a non-profit that teaches high school students professional and leadership skills through transformational weeklong in-school workshops and summer camps. As a Company Advisor I work with a team of 8 - 12 students providing them support and feedback as they go through the process.

  • Northwest Education Access, Board Member & Treasurer

    2019 - 2024

    Northwest Education Access (NWEdA) is a non-profit with the mission of providing comprehensive and individualized support to help low-income young people, ages 16-29, build their own path to higher education and beyond. As a volunteer, board member, and later board treasurer, I helped the organization in their mission providing support in their programs, budgets, fundraising, and governance.

  • United Way of King County, VITA Tax Preparer

    2021

    VITA is a program by the US Federal Government to provide free tax preparation to low-to-medium income tax payers, helping many claim underutilized tax credits like the Earned Income Tax Credit. As a tax preparer, I helped people navigate the confusing tax system and file their taxes.

  • National Association of Colleges and Employers, STEM Recruiting Trends Subgroup Lead

    2020 - 2021

    NACE is a consortium of colleges, career centers, and employers, connecting members of the early talent world. Leading the STEM Recruiting Trends Subgroup, I worked to support practitioners as they navigated the STEM hiring market in the face of a rapidly changing world in COVID.

  • JA Business Week, Company Advisor

    2018 - 2019

    Junior Achievement Business Week was a program by Junior Achievement Rocky Mountain, inspired by Washington Business Week, teaching high school students business and professional skills. An alumnus of the program, I chose to join as a Company Advisor helping a team of 8 - 16 students as they worked with real-world businesses to create and pitch new products.

  • Junior Achievement of Washington, Classroom Volunteer

    2011 - 2013

    Junior Achievement teaches K-12 students the skills they need to be successful in the workplace. As a classroom volunteer, I taught pre-prepared lessons about business and personal finance in 3rd - 5th grade classrooms.
